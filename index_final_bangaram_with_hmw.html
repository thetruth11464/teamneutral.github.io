01 — Problem Statement

A Crisis Hidden
in Plain Sight

India's JEE coaching industry has created a high-pressure residential ecosystem at Narayana and Sri Chaitanya institutions that systematically destroys student mental health — while being celebrated as a path to success.

Every year, hundreds of thousands of 15–19 year old students from across India relocate to residential coaching hubs. They are placed in overcrowded hostels, subjected to mandatory 6 AM–10 PM schedules, stripped of phones, personal freedom, and identity — all in pursuit of an All India Rank in JEE.

"This isn't just exam stress anymore — it's ongoing anxiety, burnout, and in some cases students feeling completely stuck or hopeless."

Our field research at Narayana Madhapur (Hyderabad) uncovered a deeply alarming pattern: institutions that were founded to educate have become engines of psychological harm. The coaching industry's commercial survival depends on rank lists — a model that treats students as output metrics, not human beings.

02 — Our Solution

The Neutral Band
Ecosystem

A screenless, phone-independent wearable that passively monitors student wellness 24/7 — paired with an institutional AI dashboard for early intervention.

Smart Wellness,
Made Simple. No Phone Required.

The Neutral Band syncs automatically with campus hubs placed in hostels, classrooms, the mess, and the library — requiring zero interaction from the student. It monitors the invisible crisis before it becomes a headline.

Burnout Prevention Flow

How It Works

01
Wear
Student wears the screenless Neutral Band seamlessly throughout their 16-hour day. No interaction required — it's always on.
02
Sync
Data syncs automatically as the student walks past campus hubs — in the cafeteria, classroom, hostel, and library. No phone, no app.
03
Detect
AI analyzes study-load, sleep debt, stress markers, and HRV trends to detect early warning signs of burnout — before they escalate.
04
Intervene
The institutional dashboard sends a discrete alert to counselors and wardens to initiate a check-in — before a crisis becomes a tragedy.

Long-Term Problems

Systemic issues that the Neutral Band's data and ecosystem can drive over time — requiring institutional culture shifts, policy change, and sustained engagement beyond the wearable itself.

Redefine Success
Shift institutional culture away from rank-centric identity. Use accumulated wellness data to make the case that holistic well-being correlates with better academic outcomes — replacing the "IIT or Nothing" mindset over time.
Physical Environment Reform
Address overcrowded hostels, poor ventilation, basement classrooms, and hygiene failures. The band's activity and sleep data creates documented evidence to pressure institutions into infrastructure investment.
Parent Education
Shift parents from being a source of pressure to active supporters. Neutral Band's weekly wellness reports to parents — focused on health, not marks — gradually reframes what "progress" means at home.
Stigma-Free Mental Health Access
Normalize counseling as a performance tool, not a crisis service. As the Neutral Band flags at-risk students proactively, counseling interactions become routine — eroding stigma over academic cohorts.
Campus Nutrition
Reform mess menus to support brain health and mood stability. Long-term nutritional data from the band's wellness scores can build the evidence base needed to justify a campus dietician and menu overhaul.
Policy & Regulatory Change
Aggregate anonymized Neutral Band data across institutions to publish annual student wellness reports — submitted to AICTE and UGC to drive mandatory mental health standards in all residential coaching institutes.

03 — Project Journey

From Observation
to Innovation

Our design thinking journey spanned weeks of field research, ideation, expert synthesis, and iterative prototyping — guided by the voices of real students who lived inside these systems.

Phase 1 · Week 1–2

Problem Discovery & News Research

Team Neutral began by aggregating news clippings from Times of India, Hindustan Times, Deccan Chronicle, and others — building a factual dossier of student suicides at Narayana and Chaitanya institutions. The sheer volume of incidents validated the urgency of the problem space.

📰 12 news articles documented
Phase 2 · Week 2–3

Field Interviews — 6 Students

We conducted structured, consent-based audio interviews with 6 former Narayana hostel students — Ashmit (NIT Raipur), Yash, Manan, B Aditya, Abhiram, and Sachin. All were 20 years old at time of interview and had completed JEE preparation under the residential system. The findings were unambiguous and deeply personal.

🎙️ 6 recorded interviews · Fully transcribed
Phase 3 · Week 3–4

Empathy Mapping & Persona Development

Using the interview data, we built a detailed empathy map for B Adithya — a representative JEE aspirant archetype. We mapped what he Says, Thinks, Does, and Feels, uncovering key contradictions: students verbalize distress yet continue because the system offers no escape and fear of failure is overwhelming.

🧠 1 full empathy map · Journey map created
Phase 4 · Week 4–5

Scholarly Literature Review

Each team member sourced and analyzed 2 peer-reviewed papers — yielding 12 scholarly articles from Indian Journal of Public Health, Indian Journal of Psychiatry, Cureus, Sage Journals, and Wiley Online Library. The academic consensus confirmed: the coaching industry is a Commercial Determinant of Health.

📚 12 scholarly papers reviewed
Phase 5 · Week 5–6

6-3-5 Ideation — 648 Ideas Generated

Using the 6-3-5 brainwriting method, all 6 team members generated ideas across 5 "How Might We" question clusters — producing 108 ideas per cluster, 648 ideas total. Themes covered redefining success, physical environments, parent education, mental health access, nutrition, and financial anxiety.

💡 648 raw ideas · 5 HMW clusters
Phase 6 · Week 6–7

Convergence & Prototype — Neutral Band

From 648 ideas, the team converged on the "Neutral Band Ecosystem" — a screenless wearable with an institutional dashboard — as the most feasible, innovative, and impactful solution. A prototype flow diagram was created mapping the Wear → Sync → Detect → Intervene pipeline.

🛠️ 1 converged prototype · Flow diagram created

Key Contradiction

Students say the system is too stressful, but continue because the system has no graceful exit. Leaving means "failing" — which is perceived as worse than enduring. This psychological trap is the core design challenge the Neutral Band addresses through institutional early intervention rather than individual resilience.

HMW Question 1

How might we broaden the definition of success for JEE students so that self-worth isn't tied solely to All India Rank?

Celebrate "Growth Milestones" — rewarding personal score improvement over rank
Invite alumni who didn't get into IIT but are highly successful in other fields
Shift weekly assemblies from "Topper Speeches" to "Student Journey" stories
Run a "Failure Resume" workshop to normalize setbacks as learning moments
Replace absolute rank lists on notice boards with percentile bands
HMW Question 2

How might we modify physical living and study environments so surroundings reduce rather than induce stress?

Designate "Movement Zones" with standing desks and yoga mats
Replace harsh fluorescent lights with adjustable task lighting in study cubicles
Install "Circadian Lighting" that shifts warm amber in evenings to regulate sleep
Create "Outdoor Study Verandas" for natural sunlight and fresh air
Establish "Device-Free Lounges" with comfortable seating and physical books
HMW Question 3

How might we educate parents of JEE aspirants so they become a source of support rather than pressure?

Implement a "Safe Call" window where parents talk about anything but studies
Distribute "Toxic Phrases to Avoid" during exam season to all parents
Require parents to take a mock JEE paper to understand the actual difficulty
Create a "Parent Pledge" to never use guilt as a motivating tool
Send "Emotional Progress Reports" to parents instead of only marks sheets
HMW Question 4

How might we make mental health counseling accessible and stigma-free so students seek help early?

Rename "Counseling Centers" to "Performance Coaching Centers" to reduce stigma
Provide anonymous AI-powered chatbots for initial mental health screening
Make the first counseling session mandatory and normal during induction week
Use QR codes on every desk linking to private counselor booking
Frame counseling as "Mind Gym" to equate it with physical fitness culture
HMW Question 5

How might we optimize campus nutrition to support brain health and mood stability?

Redesign mess menu around "Brain Foods" — omega-3s, nuts, leafy greens
Distribute "Nut Trails" (almonds, walnuts) before major exams as cognitive support
Stop serving heavy oil-laden meals before afternoon classes to prevent sleepiness
Hire a full-time dietician to audit mess menu weekly
Remove all energy drinks from campus; replace with herbal tea stations

08 — Challenges & Learnings

What We Faced,
What We Learned

Authentic design thinking is messy. These are the real challenges our team encountered — and how we navigated them.

01
Gaining Student Trust for Sensitive Interviews
Convincing former students to discuss mental health and institutional trauma on record was difficult. Many were hesitant about privacy and whether their accounts would be used responsibly.
→ Solution: Developed a formal consent protocol with guarantees of confidentiality. Conducted interviews in a conversational, non-clinical format. All 6 participants gave explicit consent before recording.
Solved
02
Emotional Weight of the Research Material
Reading multiple news articles about student deaths and processing interview recordings about psychological harm was emotionally taxing for team members. Maintaining research objectivity while caring deeply about the problem was a genuine tension.
→ Lesson: The emotional weight was also our strength — it kept the team human-centered. We built in debrief moments after heavy research sessions.
Insight
03
Managing 648 Ideas Without Losing Good Ones
The 6-3-5 method produced 648 ideas — a volume that made synthesis and convergence genuinely difficult. Early convergence sessions felt arbitrary, and the team worried about discarding valuable ideas prematurely.
→ Solution: Ran two separate convergence rounds — first by theme, then by feasibility/impact matrix. Built an idea log to preserve non-selected ideas for future phases.
Solved
04
Designing for a Phone-Restricted Environment
The core constraint — students cannot have smartphones in the Narayana system — eliminated most digital solution categories. App-based, SMS-based, and web-based interventions were all ruled out. This was a major design constraint that felt initially limiting.
→ Insight: The constraint became the innovation. A phone-free wearable that syncs passively was uniquely possible because of this restriction, not despite it. The Neutral Band is more privacy-preserving and less distracting than any app could be.
Insight
05
Balancing Systemic Critique with Actionable Design
The research made clear that the coaching industry itself is the problem — but we cannot design away an entire economic and cultural system. Early proposals were either too utopian (abolish coaching institutes) or too incremental (add a poster about stress).
→ Learning: The best design occupies the "adjacent possible" — working within existing constraints to shift outcomes. The Neutral Band doesn't require institutions to change their culture first; it creates data that over time drives that change.
Insight
06
Ensuring Research Rigor Across 12 Diverse Papers
With each team member sourcing different papers in different journals, maintaining consistent analysis frameworks and ensuring non-overlapping coverage required coordination. Early summaries varied widely in depth.
→ Solution: Standardized a summary template: Title, Author, Year, Key Findings, Methodology, Conclusion. Used shared documentation for cross-referencing and preventing duplication.
Solved
09 — Future Scope

What Comes
Next

The Neutral Band ecosystem is a prototype concept with clear pathways to production, scale, and systemic impact across India's coaching landscape.

🔬
Hardware Prototyping

Develop a working hardware prototype of the Neutral Band using off-the-shelf biometric sensor modules (MAX30102 for HR/SpO2, EDA sensors, accelerometers). Validate sensor accuracy for stress prediction in real hostel conditions. Target: a wearable with 7-day battery life, IP67 rating, and BLE sync capability.

🤖
AI Model Development

Train a burnout prediction model on aggregated wellness data from a pilot cohort. Use federated learning to keep individual data on-device while contributing to batch-level trend models. Integrate with an institutional dashboard built on a role-based access system for counselors, wardens, and administrators.

🏫
Pilot Program — 1 Institution

Partner with a single Narayana or Chaitanya campus to pilot a 3-month deployment with 200 volunteer students. Measure: sleep quality improvement, counselor utilization rate, self-reported stress levels, and (long-term) academic performance correlation. Publish findings in an academic journal.

📈
Scale Across Coaching Hubs

Expand to Kota, Pune, and Chennai coaching clusters. Build a SaaS dashboard product for institutional licensing. Integrate with existing student management software (ERP systems used by Narayana/Chaitanya). Target: 10,000 students monitored by Year 3.

⚖️
Policy Advocacy

Use aggregated anonymized data from the Neutral Band network to produce annual "Student Wellness Reports" submitted to AICTE, UGC, and state education boards. Make the case for mandatory mental health monitoring in all residential coaching institutes — shifting policy through evidence.

🌐
Open Research Platform

Publish the full anonymized dataset from the pilot program as an open research resource. Create an API for mental health researchers to access aggregated coaching-student wellness trends. Partner with IIT and IISc labs working on educational psychology and adolescent health.
